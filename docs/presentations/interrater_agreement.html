Core tension: "whether the participant has difficulty walking" is a better sentence than "difficulty walking" — but the framing is shared across 200+ CDEs and adds noise to embeddings.

Stripping Optimizes for Discrimination

  • Stripped text doesn't need to be grammatically correct — it needs to be distinct from other CDEs
  • Shared framing like "whether the participant has" makes 200+ CDEs look similar in embedding space
  • Noun-phrase boilerplate ("Scale of", "Score for") is more damaging than functional framing — it looks like content to embedding models
Empirical finding (synthetic data): Descriptive verbosity pushed CDEs to cluster periphery but did not cause misclassification. Stripping should tighten clusters without breaking them.

Clustering: Key Findings

  • MD consistently worst silhouette in both models (0.546, 0.562) — over-stripping degrades cluster separation
  • GT achieves highest silhouette on bl embedding (0.602) — targeted stripping improves clustering over baseline
  • On cm embedding, baseline wins (0.617) — optimal stripping level is embedding-model dependent
  • GT on cm has lowest noise (170) and highest coverage (88.9%) — best at keeping CDEs in clusters
Over-stripping is worse than no stripping. MD's 1,022 patterns remove enough discriminative content that CDEs become unclassifiable noise.
Optimal window: GT's 280 targeted patterns sit near the sweet spot — enough to reduce boilerplate noise, not enough to destroy discriminative signal.

90%+ of lost CDEs retain 70%+ of their text. They lost shared boilerplate (e.g., "During the past 7 days") that HDBSCAN was using as density signal — not discriminative semantic content.
Example:
BL: During the past 7 days: I feel tired
GT: : I feel tired  →  14 chars remain (39% retention) — content intact, temporal prefix removed

Recovery Options

  1. Post-clustering nearest-centroid assignment
    • Noise points within a distance threshold assigned to nearest cluster
    • Most promising: 90% of lost CDEs have intact content and likely sit near existing centroids
  2. Lower min_cluster_size in HDBSCAN
    • Lost CDEs are near clusters but below density threshold
  3. Accept the split
    • “I feel tired” vs “I have energy” are semantically distinct
    • Stripping revealed they were clustered together for the wrong reason (shared temporal prefix)
Option 3 is arguably correct: the boilerplate was an artificial clustering signal. Stripping reveals the true semantic structure.
